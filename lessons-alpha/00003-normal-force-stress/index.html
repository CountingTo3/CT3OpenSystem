 Stress due to normal forces is the first of three main types of stress and the easiest to calculate. Given normal force P acting perpendicular to a cross-sectional area A, the normal stress (denoted by σ) due to this force is given below.

σF = PA

 The subscript F refers to the fact that this normal stress is due to a force. Normal stresses can also occur due to an applied moment.

St. Venant's Principle

 St. Venant's Principle states that for large enough distances in away from a concentrated loading force, the stress on a cross-sectional area of the object is essentially equivalent everywhere and is equal to the force over area, as stated above. The distance at which the force gets dispersed across a material varies, but often, it will be equally distributed at a distance of one width away.
